Composer Review 2025 — Build Automated Trading Strategies Without Writing Code

Composer lets you describe a trading strategy in plain English and it builds it for you — automatically. We ran it with real money for 30 days to see if it holds up.

Systematic trading — building rules-based strategies that execute automatically — used to require either a quant background or an expensive hedge fund subscription. Composer is trying to change that.

The pitch: describe your strategy in plain English ("buy SPY when it's above its 200-day moving average, sell when it falls below"), and Composer builds it, backtests it, and runs it automatically with real money. No coding required.

We opened an account, funded it with $5,000, ran three strategies for 30 days, and came back with an honest take.

How It Actually Works

Composer is built around "symphonies" — their term for automated trading strategies. You can build one from scratch using their visual editor, describe one in natural language and let the AI build it, or browse their marketplace of pre-built strategies from other users.

Once your symphony is live, it runs on autopilot. Trades execute automatically based on your rules. You can monitor performance in real time but the system doesn't need your input.

Key feature: Before going live, every strategy gets backtested against historical data so you can see how it would have performed over the past 1, 3, and 5 years. Backtesting isn't perfect, but it's a crucial sanity check.

Our 30-Day Results

We ran three strategies — a momentum strategy, a volatility-based allocation strategy, and one from the marketplace. Results were mixed but instructive:

  • The momentum strategy slightly underperformed a simple S&P 500 buy-and-hold during our test period
  • The volatility strategy performed well during a choppy two-week stretch in the middle of our test
  • The marketplace strategy was the standout — up 4.2% vs 2.1% for SPY in the same period

30 days is too short to draw meaningful conclusions. But the platform worked exactly as advertised — strategies executed correctly, fees were transparent, and the interface was easy to navigate.
