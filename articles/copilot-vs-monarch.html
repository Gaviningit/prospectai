Copilot vs Monarch Money — The Best Mint Replacement in 2025

Both use AI to automatically track your finances. Both replaced Mint. But they're built for completely different types of people. Here's which one is right for you.

ProspectAI Editorial

When Mint shut down in early 2024, millions of users needed a new personal finance app. Two platforms rose to the top: Copilot (iOS-first, design-forward, AI-powered) and Monarch Money (cross-platform, feature-rich, built for couples and power users).

We used both for 60 days — tracking real spending, real investments, and real budgets — to find out which one actually earns your $10-$15/month.

Quick Comparison

Choose Copilot if: You're on iPhone, want the most beautiful app, and value AI-powered automatic categorization above all else.

Choose Monarch if: You're on Android or web, managing finances with a partner, or want the most features and customization.

The Verdict

Our Verdict

iPhone user? Copilot. Everyone else? Monarch.

If you're on iPhone and want the best possible experience, Copilot's AI and design are worth the price. If you're on Android, share finances with a partner, or want maximum control over your budget, Monarch is the better choice. Both offer free trials — try them both for a week and see which one you actually open every day.
