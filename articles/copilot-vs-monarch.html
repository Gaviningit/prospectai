Copilot vs Monarch Money — The Best Mint Replacement in 2025

Both use AI to automatically track your finances across every account. Both are the top Mint replacements on the market. But they're built for very different people. Here's which one is actually right for you.

ProspectAI Editorial

When Mint shut down in January 2024, it left millions of users needing a new personal finance app. Two platforms quickly rose to the top: Copilot Money and Monarch Money.

Both apps automatically sync your bank accounts, credit cards, and investments. Both use AI to categorize your spending. Both have earned strong reviews. But they take meaningfully different approaches — and picking the wrong one wastes both money and time.

We used both apps daily for 60 days with real accounts to give you a straight answer.

Bottom line up front: Copilot is the better app if you're on Apple devices and want the most beautiful, AI-driven experience. Monarch is better if you're on Android, share finances with a partner, or want maximum control and customization.

Copilot Money — The Smart, Elegant Choice

Copilot Money
★★★★★ 4.8 / 5

Copilot is built around one core strength: AI that learns how you spend money and gets smarter over time. It automatically tags every transaction, detects subscriptions you forgot about, and surfaces spending trends with minimal input from you. Available on iPhone, iPad, Mac, and Web.

Pros
  • Best AI transaction categorization available
  • Learns your habits — gets more accurate weekly
  • Tracks spending, investments, and net worth
  • Catches forgotten subscriptions automatically
  • Beautiful, fast interface across all Apple devices
Cons
  • No Android app (Apple ecosystem only)
  • No collaborative features for couples
  • $7.92/month ($95/year) — no free tier

Monarch Money — The Complete, Flexible Choice

Monarch Money
★★★★☆ 4.6 / 5

Monarch connects to 13,000+ financial institutions and works on every platform — iOS, Android, and web. Its standout features are collaborative tools (partners and financial advisors can access your account at no extra cost) and deep customization for budgets, goals, and reports.

Pros
  • Works on iOS, Android, and web — every device
  • Partner and advisor access at no extra cost
  • Connects to 13,000+ financial institutions
  • Highly customizable budgets and goal tracking
  • 7-day free trial available
Cons
  • Interface is functional but less polished than Copilot
  • AI categorization less accurate than Copilot
  • Can feel overwhelming to set up initially

Pricing Head-to-Head

  • Copilot: $7.92/month billed annually ($95/year). No free tier — free trial available before connecting accounts.
  • Monarch: Pricing starts lower than Copilot for annual plans. 7-day free trial, no credit card required. Partners get access included at no extra cost.
Our Verdict

iPhone user flying solo? Copilot. Everyone else? Monarch.

Copilot's AI is genuinely the best in the budgeting category — if you're on Apple devices and want an app that practically runs itself, it's worth every penny. If you're on Android, managing money with a partner, or want deeper control over your financial data, Monarch is the more complete solution. Both offer trials — try them both before committing.
